Region of the State

According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 149.60 square miles (387.5 km2), of which 148.54 square miles (384.7 km2) is land and 1.07 square miles (2.8 km2) (0.72%) is water. The Neuse River flows through the northeastern corner of the city. Raleigh is located in the northeast central region of North Carolina, where the Piedmont and Atlantic coastal plain regions meet. This area is known as the "fall line" because it marks the elevation inland at which waterfalls begin to appear in creeks and rivers. As a result, most of Raleigh features gently rolling hills that slope eastward toward the state's flat coastal plain. The city of Raleigh is located 24 mi (39 km) southeast of Durham; 63 mi (101 km) northeast of Fayetteville; 131 mi (211 km) northwest of Wilmington; and 155 mi (249 km) southwest of Richmond, Virginia; and 165 mi (266 km) northeast of Charlotte. A small portion of Raleigh is located in Durham County, North Carolina.

Average Income Compared to the Rest of the State

There are 462,219 residents in Raleigh, with a median age of 34.2. Of this, 48.62% are males and 51.38% are females. US-born citizens make up 85.44% of the resident pool in Raleigh, while non-US-born citizens account for 6.82%. Additionally, 7.75% of the population is represented by non-citizens. A total of 365,152 people in Raleigh currently live in the same house as they did last year.

The Warehouse District, situated in downtown Raleigh, North Carolina, is a significant urban area known for its vibrant arts scene, diverse dining options, bustling nightlife, and thriving entrepreneurial spirit. Positioned just three blocks west of the Raleigh Convention Center, this district is characterized by six blocks of expansive red-brick buildings. Most of these structures have been repurposed from their original use as warehouses, factories, and depots, contributing to the district's unique atmosphere and industrial charm.
