Region of the State

According to data from the United States Census Bureau, Raleigh spans a total area of 149.60 square miles (387.5 km2), with 148.54 square miles (384.7 km2) being land and 1.07 square miles (2.8 km2) (0.72%) being water. The Neuse River flows through the northeastern corner of the city. Situated in the northeast central region of North Carolina, Raleigh lies at the junction of the Piedmont and Atlantic coastal plain regions, known as the "fall line." This designation marks the elevation inland where waterfalls start to appear in creeks and rivers. Consequently, Raleigh predominantly features gently rolling hills that slope eastward towards the state's flat coastal plain. Raleigh's strategic location places it 24 miles (39 km) southeast of Durham, 63 miles (101 km) northeast of Fayetteville, 131 miles (211 km) northwest of Wilmington, 155 miles (249 km) southwest of Richmond, Virginia, and 165 miles (266 km) northeast of Charlotte. Additionally, a small portion of Raleigh extends into Durham County, North Carolina.

Classification

In Raleigh, the population stands at 462,219 residents, with a median age of 34.2. Of this population, 48.62% are males and 51.38% are females. US-born citizens constitute 85.44% of the resident pool, while non-US-born citizens make up 6.82%. Additionally, 7.75% of the population consists of non-citizens. Furthermore, a total of 365,152 individuals in Raleigh currently reside in the same house as they did last year.

Located in downtown Raleigh, North Carolina, the Warehouse District stands out as a prominent urban area renowned for its lively arts scene, eclectic dining choices, vibrant nightlife, and flourishing entrepreneurial culture. Nestled just three blocks west of the Raleigh Convention Center, this district spans six blocks adorned with expansive red-brick buildings. Many of these edifices have undergone transformation from their original roles as warehouses, factories, and depots, adding to the district's distinctive ambiance and industrial allure.
