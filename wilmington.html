Year City Was Incorporated

The city was founded in the 1730s, and after going through a series of different names (New Carthage, New London, Newton), its name became Wilmington in 1740, named after Spencer Compton, 1st Earl of Wilmington.

Region of the State

Wilmington is the eastern terminus of Interstate 40, an east-west freeway that ends 2,554 miles away at Barstow, California, where it joins I-15, the gateway to Southern California. This road passes through many major cities and state capitals along the way. According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 52.97 square miles (137.2 km2), of which 51.41 square miles (133.2 km2) is land and 1.56 square miles (4.0 km2) (2.95%) is water. Wrightsville Beach is a common destination in the Wilmington area. Carolina Beach and Kure Beach also add to the city's attractions.

Average Income Compared to the Rest of the State

The median household income in Wilmington, NC in 2022 was $63,835, which was 5.7% less than the median annual income of $67,481 across the entire state of North Carolina. Compared to the median income of $31,099 in 2000 this represents an increase of 51.3%. The per capita income in 2022 was $43,229, which means an increase of 50.3% compared to 2000 when it was $21,503.

Stretching across nearly two miles, the Riverwalk in Wilmington serves as much more than a picturesque pathway. Originating from an idea conceived in the 1980s to revitalize Wilmington's historic downtown, it has since become the pulsating core of the city. Lined with eateries providing outdoor dining experiences, charming boutique shops, and regular events, the Riverwalk encapsulates Wilmington's lively atmosphere. Moreover, it provides uninterrupted vistas of the scenic Cape Fear River, adding to its allure as a must-visit destination in the city..
