North Carolina History

North Carolina, one of the original 13 colonies, holds the distinction of being the first state to direct its delegates to endorse independence from the British crown during the Continental Congress. Post the Revolutionary War, North Carolina fostered a robust slave plantation system and emerged as a key exporter of cotton and tobacco. Despite this economic growth, the enslaved population in North Carolina remained comparatively smaller than in other southern states..

In 1861, North Carolina joined 10 other states in seceding from the United States, marking the beginning of the American Civil War. Despite the absence of significant battles on its soil, North Carolina contributed more recruits to the Confederate cause than any other southern state. Then, in 1903, the state gained historical significance as the location of the first manned self-propelled airplane flight, accomplished by the Wright brothers as they took off from a cliff near Kitty Hawk.
