According to data from the United States Census Bureau, Charlotte spans a total area of 312.00 square miles (808.1 km2). Of this, 310.02 square miles (802.9 km2) constitutes land, while 1.98 square miles (5.1 km2) (0.63%) comprises water bodies. Positioned at an elevation of 751 feet (229 m), Charlotte ranks as the twenty-sixth-largest city in the United States. Situated within the Carolina Piedmont, Charlotte predominantly occupies Mecklenburg County. Uptown Charlotte, situated between Sugar Creek and Irwin Creek, stands on the grounds formerly occupied by the St. Catherine's and Rudisill gold mines. Charlotte's geographic proximity places it 25 miles (40 km) southwest of Concord, 26 miles (42 km) northeast of Rock Hill, South Carolina, 83 miles (134 km) southwest of Greensboro, 135 miles (217 km) west of Fayetteville, and 165 miles (266 km) southwest of Raleigh, the state capital. Unlike many cities of similar size, Charlotte's development has not been significantly impacted by nearby bodies of water or geographical features, facilitating its growth as a transportation hub for highways, railways, and air travel.

In 2022, Charlotte, NC, boasted a median household income of $74,401, surpassing the state-wide median of $67,481 by 9.3%. This marks a notable increase of 36.9% from the median income of $46,975 recorded in 2000. Additionally, the per capita income for Charlotte in 2022 stood at $49,498, exhibiting a substantial growth of 45.8% from its 2000 counterpart of $26,823.

Romare Bearden Park, spanning 5.4 acres, is a public park situated at 300 S. Church Street in Charlotte, North Carolina. Named after the renowned artist Romare Bearden, a native of Charlotte, the park officially opened its doors in late August 2013. Situated across from Truist Field, the home stadium of the International League's Charlotte Knights, the park is located in Uptown Charlotte and hosts a variety of fitness and cultural arts programs throughout the year. Inspired by Bearden's collages and paintings, the park's design was crafted by supervising artist Norie Sato. Among its features are two gardens, a courtyard with dining tables and chairs, a formal event green field, a play area featuring interactive digital chimes, including dance chimes, and several waterfalls. The grand opening celebration occurred on August 31-September 1, 2013.
